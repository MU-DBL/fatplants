Information

Introduction

Fatplants is a web portal with analysis and visualization tools for plant fat-related genes, proteins and metabolism. It empowers researchers to develop and test hypotheses, and design crops with value-added compositions. In particular, collating data in Fatplants on oilseed engineering through can accelerate the process of knowledge discovery and crop design for biology researchers with little computer skills. For example, increasing seed oil content by plant breeding has resulted in trade-offs or penalties with respect to protein content, seed size, or seed set, and FatPlants provides useful information to address this issue.

We have developed this “one-stop-shop” community web resource using various data of Glycine max, Arabidopsis thaliana, and Camelina sativa generated from our studies, curated from the public sources, and collected from the literature to help understand metabolism synthesis pathways and related protein interactions. Some data and annotations come from Uniprot, TAIR, LIPIDMAPS, OPSIN, LMPD, PlantFAdb and ARALIPS. FatPlants also includes gene and protein information cards, Blast search function, and similar structure search function.

Site Features

Data
  • Integrated data of fatty acid-related proteins, genes for Arabidopsis, Soybean and Camellia.
  • Functional information for each protein, including the modification region, post-translation modification or functional structure region.
  • Physicochemical information of fatty acids.
Analysis
  • Sequence similarity search based on Blastp against the fatty-acid related proteins.
  • 3D-structure similarity search and visualization using in-house G2S against the fatty-acid related proteins.
  • Pathway mapping to KEGG and Reactome.
  • Collection of fatty-acid related pathway pictures from the literature and interactive visualization using our in-house machine learning image understanding tool.
Visualization
  • Visualization of protein-protein interaction for fatty-related proteins.
  • Visualization of GO Enrichment network for fatty acid related proteins.
